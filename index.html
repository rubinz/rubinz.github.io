About Me

I am a Computer Science Major interested in the development of aerial solutions. I have been building multirotors on my own and in makerspaces for the last four years and am excited to see how Multirotors can make the world a better place.

Passion

I started building drones as a hobby. The satisfaction from making something capable of flight from nothing is unbelievable. As my involvement in the hobby has grown I have seen the great potential it has for the world.

Education

I started my Computer Science BA in 2011 and will be graduating in Spring 2016. At Wheaton College I have been exposed to the well rounded education a liberal arts school provides as well as having full access to an awesome maker spaces.

Videos

One of the early projects I started was a YouTube channel to document my projects and progress as a pilot. My work with multirotors lead me into learning about aerial filming and mapping, which has lead me to develop a broad piloting skillset.

Experience

Data Visualization Research

I worked with the head of the Computer Science department over the summer on visualizing ranked, time-series data using graphic displays and a clustering display. The majority of the work was replicating a Flash Action Script version using Javascript and HTML5.

Tech Support Student Manager

I currently hold the senior most student position in the college tech support department. I have extensive experience with basic computer functionality and trouble shooting as well as proficiency with Filemaker Pro, Deep Freeze, and bulk computer imaging.

Emergency Training Aerial Recon

I have had the opportunity to accompany the Bristol County technical rescue team to a number of training exercises and scenarios. Access to fast and accurate aerial inspection and recon was incredibly valuable to the response team.

Aerial Video

I have worked indevidually as well as with the Wheaton College communications department and Film and New Media Studies department to help promote a positive view of drones and their capability through aerial footage and images, panel talks, and flight training sessions.
